How to Protect Yourself

Protect yourself and others around you by knowing the facts and taking appropriate precautions. Follow advice provided by your local health authority.

Wear Mask

Masks can help prevent the spread of the virus from the person wearing the mask to others. And should be combined with physical distancing and hand hygiene. Wear a mask when physical distancing is not possible.

Wash Your Hands

Washing hands can keep you healthy and prevent the spread of respiratory and diarrheal infections from one person to the next.Handwashing is one of the best way to protect your whole family.

Stay at Home

The COVID-19 pandemic means that many of us are staying at home and doing less in terms of social interactions and exercise. This can have a negative effect on your physical and mental health.

Coronavirus Cases

B.1.351 South Africa

Beta - This one, which appeared in South Africa in August 2020, led to a resurgence in Covid cases that overwhelmed southern Africa. It’s been reported in at least 122 countries. The South African variant carries a mutation, called N501Y, that appears to make it more contagious or easy to spread.The South Africa variant causes more serious illness for the vast majority of people.

Read More

B.1.617.2 India

Delta - This fast-spreading variant stoked a dramatic wave of Covid cases in India that overwhelmed hospitals and crematoriums and has since been found in at 104 countries. It’s estimated to be 55% more transmissible than alpha and almost twice as infectious as the original strain that began spreading worldwide in early 2020. Doctors in India have linked to delta.

Read More

B.1.1.7 England

Alpha - This variant emerged in England in September 2020 and drove a winter surge in cases that sent the U.K. back into lockdown in January. Other countries, particularly in Europe, followed the U.K. in reimposing movement restrictions. Alpha was previously the dominant strain in the U.S., and has been reported in at least 173 countries, according to the WHO.
